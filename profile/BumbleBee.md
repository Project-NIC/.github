# NIC-FPLG — Teaser

*For Show HN, r/embedded, Hackaday and similar communities.*

---

A conventional combustion engine drags along a crankshaft, camshafts,
transfer ports in the cylinder wall, and an exhaust pipe tuned to one RPM.
The result is a machine that works beautifully in a narrow band and happily
compromises everywhere else. NIC-FPLG takes a different road.

Two pistons on a shared rod. A tubular linear generator at the rod's centre.
No crankshaft, no valvetrain. The engine runs permanently at one operating
point — in mechanical resonance set by the physical system, not by electronics.
Power output is not changed by throttling the engine; it is changed by setting
the battery charge current, which buffers and smooths the whole system.

Functions that a conventional engine solves with moving parts are handled here
by geometry: drop-shaped pockets in the piston face direct the flow and spin
the charge, valve inertia delays transfer timing so the exhaust leaves first.
Stratified charge without high-pressure direct injection. Exhaust port
decarbonisation without disassembly. Generator as a sealed-for-life cartridge.

The principles hold. The numbers don't exist yet — they are waiting on
simulations, calculations, and a prototype. This repo is a brief, not a result.
We are looking for people who can say "I don't know, but I'd try this" —
and then try it.
